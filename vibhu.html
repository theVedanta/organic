What is Organic Farming?

Organic farming is a technique, which involves the cultivation of plants and rearing of animals in natural ways. This process involves the use of biological materials, avoiding synthetic substances to maintain soil fertility and ecological balance thereby minimizing pollution and wastage. Also, no genetically modified organisms are permitted.

Organic farming relies on ecologically balanced agricultural principles like crop rotation, green manure, organic waste, biological pest control, mineral and rock additives. Organic farming makes use of pesticides and fertilizers if they are considered natural and avoids the use of various petrochemical fertilizers and pesticides.

Components of Organic Farming

  1. Fertilizer Management
  2. Weed Control
  3. Insect pest and disease control
  4. Artificial fertilizers are banned in organic farming
  5. Organic farmers use animal manure, compost and human sewage.
  6. Worms, insets and bacteria underground are always working on making soil good.
  7. By using the process of crop rotation, the farmer can keep a good soil for many years.
